About

HackState is a hackathon hosted at Mississippi State University. Over the duration of 24 hours, students are immersed in tech culture and encouraged to exercise their imagination to create interesting and innovative hacks. Come to have fun with your friends, learn something new, and have a generally awesome time. HackState is diverging from the typical hackathon by becoming the first Major League Hacking hackathon in Mississippi.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is sprint-like coding competition. Over the course of 24 hours, hackers design and build web applications, mobile applications, and hardware innovations.

Where is HackState located?

HackState is located in the Colvard Student Union at Mississippi State University.

Where can I park?

Park anywhere shown on page 10 of the Participant Guide, or simply navigate to this parking lot and walk to the Colvard Student Union.

Who can participate?

All undergraduate students and graduate students at least 18 years old. If you have graduated within one year, you are allowed to attend as well!

What if I am beginner?

This is the perfect opportunity to learn something new! There will be workshops geared towards beginners and mentors to help you throughout the event.

What if I don't have a team or idea?

You can find a team and idea at our brainstorming and team finding event.

How big can teams be? Can I hack alone?

Teams can be up to four people. Hacking alone is allowed, but we encourage you to step out of your comfort zone.

Does it cost money?

HackState is completely free to attend. In addition, food, beverages, and sleeping arrangements will be provided free of cost.

Where can I sleep?

On Saturday night we will providing sleeping arrangements for students inside the Union.

Will food be provided?

Yes. Meals, snacks, and beverages will be provided for free during HackState.

What do I need to bring?

Make sure to bring your laptop, any hardware for your hack, a sleeping bag, college ID, state ID, toiletries and a great attitude.

Is there a Code of Conduct?

Yes. Read it. Violation of Code of Conduct will result in leaving HackState and banned from attending in future years. Other disciplinary action may be taken.
